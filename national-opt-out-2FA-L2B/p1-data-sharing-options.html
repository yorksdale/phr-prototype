Setting your health data sharing options

Information about your healthcare is used and shared between healthcare professionals when you are treated or given support.

Changing your data sharing options will not affect that.

Your personal identifiable data may also be used in two additional ways:


Part 1: Research

For medical research to improve treatment and care

Your information may be used to support research and advance treatment and care.

This means:

  • Researchers can improve how diseases such as cancer are treated and prevented
  • To evaluate the quality of services provided by charities, for example, for people living with long term conditions such as dementia and diabetes

Part 2: Planning

For managing the NHS and social care services

Your information may be used to help plan and manage the NHS and social care services

This means:

  • The NHS can ask your opinion about the care you receive
  • The NHS can check the quality of the care that you receive


Who uses your data?

Organisations using your data include:

  • Hospital trusts, universities and charities
  • Pharmacies and opticians
  • Local authorities and social care providers
  • Acute trusts

If you don't want personal identifiable health data to be used for either of the above you can choose to opt out.
