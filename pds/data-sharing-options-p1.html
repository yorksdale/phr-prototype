Setting your health data sharing options

The people who deliver NHS services need to know enough about you to be able to treat you safely and effectively. The data they use to do this can also help in the treatment of other people, to check on the standard of care we provide, and to aid medical research. You can choose how we use your data.

You can choose how we use your data.

At the moment information about your healthcare is used when you are treated or given support by a health or care professional. That will continue.

You won't need to do anything further if you are satisfied how the NHS use your information.


This short video sets out how your patient record information is used and what your options are.

 

Your personal confidential information will only be used for purposes that benefit treatment and care. It will never be used for marketing or insurance purposes.


Please consider the 2 ways in which your data is used.


1

Allow your information to support research and advance treatment and care.

This means:

  • Researchers can improve how diseases such as cancer are treated and prevented
  • Charities can evaluate the quality of their services, for example for people living with dementia

 


2

Allow my information to be used to run the NHS and social care system

This means:

  • The NHS can ask your opinion about the care you have received
  • The NHS can check the quality of the care that you receive
